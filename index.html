Fretboard Codex

Fretboard Codex is a free, theory-driven guitar tool that maps music theory directly onto an interactive fretboard. It's built for guitarists who want to understand what they're playing, not just memorize shapes.

What you can explore

  • Scales & modes — major, natural/harmonic/melodic minor, and the seven modes (Ionian, Dorian, Phrygian, Lydian, Mixolydian, Aeolian, Locrian), shown live across the fretboard in any key.
  • Diatonic chords — every chord built from the scale, with roman numerals and scale-degree names (tonic, supertonic, mediant, and so on).
  • Chord voicings — playable shapes with extensions (9, 11, 13) and the resulting chord spelling.
  • Circle of fifths — key relationships and signatures at a glance.
  • Modal interchange — borrowed chords from parallel modes, labeled with accurate theory terms (Neapolitan, flat mediant, minor subdominant, and more).

Fretboard Codex runs entirely in your browser, works offline once loaded, and is free to use. Created by afterosmosis.
